I keep researching while trying to learn how to invest my 10€ I put aside every month. Everyone I asked tells me different things: real estate, funds, commodity, bitcoin, stocks, bonds, saving accounts, all are on the table.

I keep thinking and reading. Yet, I still can't figure it out.

The money is too tiny to turn into real estate.

Bonds don't bring much, they say.

Have you seen bitcoin lately?

Commodity feels weird and I don't know what makes it different than bitcoin (also in gold color, coincidentally?).

Funds and stocks are what is left for me to look at. To be able to invest in stocks, I need to have basic knowledge of accounting and bookkeeping, finance, economics and psychology. Each of those disciplines by themselves that I can't learn from ChatGPT or Reddit. I mean, I can, but even then, ChatGPT can't pick a stock for me. I have to put the hours in to evaluate each stock individually.

I'm not even getting into income and capital tax world for any of those. The more I look, the more I find building things easier—at least something I am familiar with. But the money becomes 20€ the next month and I seriously consider investing into something beneficial. But who has time to learn everything in that world?

The more I look at, the more I think all of these are scam and only people getting rich are the intermediaries telling people like me to "do X" or provide "financial services" under some umbrella.

I don't trust intermediaries as their incentive is increasing their income, not my financial value or my portfolio.

I really wish our educational system taught about these in the school. But I also know I wouldn't listen to it as I had zero money to invest.

I don't know. I have no solution. Investing in index funds seems most viable option for now. 🤷🏼
